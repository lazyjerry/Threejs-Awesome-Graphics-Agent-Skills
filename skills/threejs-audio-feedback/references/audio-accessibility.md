# Audio accessibility

Audio can reinforce information but should not be the only way to receive essential state.

## Provide

- visual indication for alerts, direction, hits, pickups, success, and failure;
- captions/transcripts for speech and meaningful non-speech content when applicable;
- volume and mute controls;
- separate categories when users need to reduce music while retaining cues;
- adjustable repetition/intensity for frequent feedback;
- no required distinction based only on pitch or stereo direction.

Avoid surprise playback before consent. Preserve control after audio is enabled.

For directional threats, combine sound with screen-space indicators, world markers, controller vibration when available, or text. For rhythm or timing-critical interactions, consider visual timing aids and configurable windows.

Test the experience muted. If the core task becomes impossible, add a non-audio channel rather than merely documenting that sound is required.
